## 2. ICP Definition

### Primary ICP: Small/Family-Owned Businesses with Manual GTM

> **Critical founder insight:** The real market is NOT agencies specifically. It is much broader -- any small business where GTM is 100% manual, data is rotting in spreadsheets, and the owner knows they should do better but lacks the time, expertise, or budget for a marketing hire.

| Attribute | Criteria |
|-----------|----------|
| **Size** | 5-50 employees, owner-operated or small management team |
| **Customer data** | In Excel/Google Sheets -- stale, no refresh mechanism |
| **CRM** | None, or using basic tools (Mailchimp, etc.) just for email blasts |
| **GTM process** | 100% manual: google a company, write an email, send, hope |
| **Enrichment** | None -- no data enrichment, no personalization, no strategy |
| **Mindset** | Know they should do better but don't have time/expertise/budget for a marketing hire |
| **Willingness to pay** | Would pay EUR 149/mo for what would otherwise be a EUR 4K/mo marketing person |
| **Decision maker** | Owner, founder, managing director (decides fast, no committee) |
| **Disqualifier** | Has a dedicated marketing team, uses enterprise tools (Salesforce, HubSpot Enterprise), or has no outbound motion at all |

#### Key Pain Points

1. **Contact data is rotting** -- people leave, companies merge, emails bounce. Their spreadsheet from 2 years ago is 40% dead data.
2. **"Outreach" means manually googling and writing one email at a time** -- 5 hours for 10 emails, with no idea if they are any good.
3. **Paying EUR 20-100/mo for Mailchimp to blast the same stale list** with generic newsletters that get 2% open rates.
4. **No tracking of what works** -- no idea which messages get replies, which segments respond, what subject lines perform.
5. **No strategy -- just activity without direction.** They are busy but not effective. Sending emails is not a GTM strategy.

#### What They Go From / To

**Before (manual GTM):**
```
Excel spreadsheet → manually Google a company → write an email from scratch →
send via Mailchimp → hope for a reply → repeat
```

**After (with the product):**
```
AI researches their market → builds outreach strategy → finds and enriches contacts →
writes hyper-personalized messages → tracks what gets replies → gets smarter every week
```

#### The Pitch

> **"Your AI marketing department for EUR 149/mo."**
>
> Not a tool. Not a database. A strategist that does the work. It researches your prospects, writes personalized outreach that references real company data, tracks what works, and gets better at it every campaign. What would cost EUR 4K/mo for a junior marketing hire costs EUR 149/mo with AI.

### Beachhead: Czech Agencies (Months 1-6)

Agencies are the ideal **beachhead segment** within the primary ICP because they check every box above AND have additional advantages for early traction:

| Attribute | Criteria |
|-----------|----------|
| **Industry** | Digital marketing, creative, event production, PR, branding agencies |
| **Size** | 5-50 employees (big enough to pay, small enough to decide fast) |
| **Revenue** | 5M-50M CZK/year |
| **Location** | Czech Republic (Prague, Brno primarily) |
| **Pain signals** | Has a messy contact book, does outbound for clients or themselves, uses spreadsheets for prospect tracking |
| **Budget** | Already paying for at least one SaaS tool (HubSpot, Mailchimp, LinkedIn Premium) |
| **Decision maker** | Founder, managing director, or head of new business |
| **Disqualifier** | Pure inbound agency (no outbound motion), fewer than 5 employees, not doing client acquisition |

**Why agencies as the beachhead:**
1. They have the pain (messy contacts, manual outreach) -- proved with United Arts
2. They understand the value of personalization (it is their business)
3. They can pay (25K CZK for a one-off proves willingness)
4. They become channel partners: once they use it for themselves, they use it for clients
5. The Czech agency market is small enough to dominate (estimated 200-400 qualifying agencies)

**But agencies are not the ceiling.** The broader primary ICP includes accounting firms, law practices, consultancies, IT service providers, real estate agencies, event companies, recruitment firms -- any owner-operated business that needs clients and currently acquires them by hand.

## 4. Pricing Strategy

### Tier Structure

| Tier | Monthly | Annual | Target User | Limits |
|------|---------|--------|------------|--------|
| **Starter** | EUR 49/mo | EUR 490/yr (2 mo free) | Solo founder, small agency (<10 people) | 50 companies/mo, 100 contacts, 200 messages |
| **Growth** | EUR 149/mo | EUR 1,490/yr (2 mo free) | Active agency, sales team | 200 companies/mo, 500 contacts, 1,000 messages |
| **Scale** | EUR 399/mo | EUR 3,990/yr (2 mo free) | Agency with multiple clients, power user | 500 companies/mo, 1,500 contacts, 3,000 messages, priority support |

### Why EUR 149 Is the Lead Tier

1. **Anchoring:** Show EUR 399 first, EUR 149 feels reasonable
2. **Unit economics:** EUR 149/mo with EUR 6 API cost = 96% gross margin
3. **Competitor context:** Apollo Pro is $79/mo with no AI. LinkedIn Sales Nav is EUR 80/mo with no enrichment. EUR 149 for research + strategy + personalized outreach is a bargain
4. **Upgrade path:** Users start at EUR 149, hit limits within 2-3 months, upgrade to EUR 399

### The United Arts Reframe

This is the single most powerful sales argument for the first 10 customers:

> "United Arts paid 25K CZK (EUR 1,000) for a one-time contact cleanup and basic outreach. For 4K CZK/month (EUR 149), you get:
> - Continuous enrichment (not one-time)
> - AI-generated personalized messages (not templates)
> - A strategy playbook that learns what works
> - New contacts researched automatically every month
>
> That is 75% less per month than what you paid for a one-time job, and it gets smarter every week."

### Discounting Rules

| Scenario | Discount | Conditions |
|----------|----------|------------|
| Annual prepay | 17% (2 months free) | Paid upfront |

| Channel partner | 33% off Growth tier | Minimum 3 seats |

**Never discount below EUR 99/mo.** Below that, the customer is not serious enough to invest time in onboarding.

## 5. Sales Playbook

### First Touch: LinkedIn Message

Use the product to generate these. The meta-value is enormous: "I used the AI to write this message to you."

**Template (customize per prospect):**

> Hi [Name],
>
> I noticed [specific observation from enrichment -- their recent client win, event they organized, new service they launched].
>
> I built an AI tool that does what I just did -- researches companies and contacts, then writes personalized outreach like this message. Agency founders in Prague are using it to book 3x more meetings.
>
> Would a 15-minute demo be worth your time? I will research your agency live and show you what the AI finds.
>
> -- Michal

**Key elements:**
- Specific observation proves the product works (you just experienced it)
- Social proof ("agency founders in Prague")
- Low commitment ask (15 minutes)
- Concrete demo promise (live research)

### Demo Script (25 minutes)

**Minutes 0-2: Context**
> "Thanks for the time. Quick question before I show you anything -- how do you currently find and reach new clients?"

Listen. Whatever they say, the demo addresses their specific pain.

**Minutes 2-7: The Flywheel (show, do not tell)**
> "Let me show you what the AI knows about your agency."

Live: enter their company domain. Show L1 enrichment results. Show what the system learned about their market, competitors, positioning. This is the "wow" moment -- they see their own company analyzed in real time.

**Minutes 7-12: Live Enrichment**
> "Now let me show you a prospect."

Pick a company from their target market (ask them: "Who is a dream client for you?"). Run L1+L2 enrichment live. Show the strategic signals, hiring patterns, news. Time it: "That took 90 seconds. How long would this take manually?"

**Minutes 12-17: Message Generation**
> "Now watch this."

Generate a personalized message for a contact at that company. Show the enrichment data that informed the message. Show the personalization hooks. Read it aloud. Ask: "Would you send this?"

**Minutes 17-22: The Strategy Layer**
> "Here is what makes this different from Apollo or Lemlist."

Show the playbook. Explain: the AI builds a strategy based on YOUR data, YOUR market, YOUR wins. And it learns: every message you edit teaches it your voice. Every reply teaches it what works.

**Minutes 22-25: Close**
> "I would like to offer you a 2-week free trial. No credit card, I personally set up your account and import your contacts. If after 2 weeks you are not booking more meetings, you walk away. Fair?"

### Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Too expensive" | "How much do you spend on manual prospect research per month? Even 5 hours at EUR 30/hour is EUR 150. This does it in minutes, continuously, and gets smarter." |
| "We do this manually and it works" | "How many personalized messages does your team send per week? 10? 20? This generates 200 in an afternoon, each referencing real company data. Manual works -- but does it scale?" |
| "We already use Apollo/HubSpot" | "Great -- keep using them. This is not a replacement for your CRM or database. This is the intelligence layer on top. It takes your contacts, researches them deeply, and writes messages your team would actually send." |
| "AI messages sound generic" | "Let me show you. [Pull up a generated message.] This references their specific hiring patterns, recent funding, and market position. That is not a template -- that is research." |
| "We need to think about it" | "Totally fair. How about this: I set up a free trial right now, import 50 of your contacts, and you see the enrichment results tomorrow morning. No commitment -- just see the data." |
| "Our contacts are a mess" | "That is actually the perfect starting point. The first thing the AI does is clean and deduplicate your contacts, then enrich them. Most agencies start with messy data -- that is feature one." |
| "What about data privacy / GDPR?" | "The system enriches from public sources only -- company websites, public registries, published news. No scraping of private data. All data stays in your account, hosted in EU (Frankfurt)." |

## Appendix B: The Agency-as-Channel Thesis

The long-term strategic play is not just selling to agencies -- it is selling THROUGH agencies:

**Phase 1 (Months 1-6):** Agencies are customers. They use the product for their own business development.

**Phase 2 (Months 7-12):** Agencies are channels. They use the product for their clients' outreach. One agency with 10 clients = 10 potential seats.

**Phase 3 (Year 2+):** Agencies are distribution. Agency partners bring their entire client roster. The product becomes infrastructure for the agency, like HubSpot is for marketing agencies.

**Revenue math for agency-as-channel:**
- 10 agency partners
- Each has 5-15 clients using the product
- Average 8 clients per agency = 80 end-user seats
- At EUR 99/seat (partner rate): EUR 7,920 MRR from partners alone
- Plus 50 direct customers at EUR 149: EUR 7,450 MRR
- Combined: EUR 15,370 MRR = EUR 184K ARR

This is how EUR 200K ARR becomes realistic without needing 75+ direct customers.
